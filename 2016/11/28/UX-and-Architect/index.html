UX and Architect

2016-11-28 archi-neering

“Always design a thing by considering it in its next larger context – a chair in a room, a room in a house, a house in an environment, an environment in a city plan.” Eero Saarinen


“A photograph is nothing more than an image created by light.” “A web application is nothing more than an experience created by design.” Tom Preston-Werner

UX in digital world == Architect in AEC world (domain)

For both of them, the final end is delivering a great Experience, Feeling. It’s people-related. In terms of visual design, the methods include manipulating FORM (proportion, perspective) and COLOR (contrast, shade). For both, you will see the fruit (product) realizing from the concepts.

UX (ba, pm, marketer, designer) Architect (ba, pm, marketer, designer)
STAGE 1: STRATEGY CONTENT PROTOTYPING STAGE 1: SCHEMATIC DESIGN (SD conceptual)
Determine project goals and requirements Determine the archi program
Competitor Analysis and Customer Analysis – It is the research phase of the project, when information gathering and planning happens. Understanding your target users, what their goals are, and what the goals of the product are…
Product Structure/Strategy (the product functions) – Certain mockups that reflect the product functional requirements are created.		 During schematic design, an architect commonly develops study drawings, documents, or other media that illustrate the concepts of the design and include spatial relationships, scale, and form for the owner to review.
Schematic design also is the research phase of the project, when zoning requirements or jurisdictional restrictions are discovered and addressed.
Deliverables: BDS TDS mockups Deliverables: Archi Programs, Archi Drawings

STAGE 2 and 3: FRONT END UI DESIGN and BACKEND DB ARCHI and DESIGN STAGE 2 and 3: DESIGN DEVELOPMENT DD and CONSTRUCTION DOCUMENTS CSD
Visual design could be strongly tied to engineering design. Both Frontend and Backend. Built-in Security etc Archi, MEP, Structural drawings, Façade Visual and Engineering details
Deliverables: UI/UX wireframe/mockups, Frontend, Backend, DB Design Docs Revised Deliverables: Detailed Drawings for construction bid and negotiation
STAGE 4: DEVELOPEMENT (usually mixed with design and same team) STAGE 4: BUILD
Shorter design period and tightly coupled with build. The process of building provides ideas for design. Small scope of iteration: backend development to make sure a certain function works. Technical… After longer design period and bidding process. If design-build method is used, it will be more similar to in house software development. However, iteration is harder due to the nature of the project. Most design has to precede build.
a large group of people or mass an individual or a certain group of people

Design-build is a method of project delivery in which one entity – the design-build team – works under a single contract with the project owner to provide design and construction services. One entity, one contract, one unified flow of work from initial concept through completion – thereby re-integrating the roles of designer and constructor. Design-build is an alternative to the traditional design-bid-build project delivery method. – dbia.org

To break down UX, it could also include experience design, interaction design etc.

The Building Analogy
The UX Designer thinks about the garden/trees, the roads in and out to access the building, distance to shops and public transport and schools, the demographics of the residents, what the rush hours for the building are and how the mail is delivered.
The interaction designer takes this data (how much data they actually take depends on the mode of work) and designs the blueprint for the building – sometimes using it to role-play scenarios delivered by the UX Designer – http://axbom.com/difference-experience-designer-interaction-designer/
