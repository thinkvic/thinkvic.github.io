  • Live and Life Will Give You Pictures

    Dec 18, 2016

    This year the two exhibitions in Barnes Foundation are great.

    One is about Picasso’s works around WWI time 1912-1924.
    “Picasso: The Great War, Experimentation and Change”
    The other is about Masterworks of French Photography 1890-1950.
    “Live and Life Will Give You Pictures”

    The former gave me insight into Picasso. Wow, we always associate him with cubism. However, he kept experimenting and changing immediately after he established cubism.
    The latter let me experience first-hand the real photographs about one of my favorite historical periods. I got into such a close contact with figures like Bresson and Brassai. Their masterworks intuitively painted the social and historical contexts of that period.
    Just like the Barnes’ new director said:

    “We like to say, ‘We’ve moved, but we didn’t unpack. We want to unpack the ideas in the works themselves. That broader social, historical, ideological context.”

  • MISSING - POEM

    Nov 19, 2016

    Last week,
    I saw your left face on the left
    in the mid night.
    Yesterday,
    I saw your left face on the left
    in the late late night.
    Today,
    I could not see you!
    Tomorrow,
    I saw your right face on the right
    in the early early night.
    Next week,
    Will I see your right face on the right
    in the mid night?
    Where are you?

  • Steve McCurry's Afghanistan

    Oct 18, 2016

    Unguarded, Untold, Iconic: Afghanistan through the Lens of Steve McCurry

    Afghanistan through the lens of McCurry is so beautiful and mystic. I feel the photographs try to grab those deep philosophical concepts. I was fascinated by both the place and the book extracts (Caravans by James A. Michener). It gave me a strong urge to visit the place and read the book. Maybe that’s the magic of art, it inspires you beyond the works itself.

    She said that we are essentially desert animals and that life is meant to be harsh. When we live in an oasis like Philadelphia or Munich, we become degenerate and lose touch with our origins.
