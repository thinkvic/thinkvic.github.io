Machine, Engine, Watt and Baldwin

Sep 20, 2014

machine has its root in GREEK meaning device.

mechanic (adj.)
Greek mekhanikos “ full of resources, inventive, “ -> LATIN mechanicus ->

machine (n.)
Greek makhana mekhane “device, means” mekhos

machine for living in…

 

engine has its root in LATIN but the meaning of mechanical device came as late as 1300.

engine (n.) / engineer
LATIN ingenium “inborn qualities, talent” -> OLD FRENCH engin “skill, cleverness” -> 1300, mechanical device

in ancient times, mechanical (device, trick) energy all originates from human labor / natural force (wind, water). starting from 1800s, other energy (chemical, fossil fuel) was converted to mechanical energy.

In 1781 James Watt patented a steam engine that produced continuous rotative motion.

Founded in 1825 in Philadelphia, Baldwin Locomotive Works was once very successful as the largest producer of steam locomotives. The photo shows the Baldwin Tower, which was built in 1928 and designed by architects Simon & Simon (it took 16 years to build).
