CHRISTMAS SHADOW - POEM

Dec 27, 2014

High noon, Christmas.
Looking outside,
sunny and quiet.
Across the road,
the Sun over the rooftop,
very low,
On the side of the road,
the shadow of a signpost,
pretty long.
Twice the post’s height?

A quick calculation in the mind.
Two numbers of altitude,
Philly and tropic of cancer.
90-23-40=27 degree.
Typing the following into Google:
tan (27 deg).
the result,
0.50952544949.
simple and elegant?
you have to admit
the beauty of God!

PS: What a coincidence. I was not aware of that Christmas happens to be close to the day of winter solstice until the calculation. Also, for me, what an experience of sublime! I stood silently, in awe of nature and its accurate proportion.

Louis Kahn (1901-1974) said: the Sun never knew how great it was until it struck the side of a building.

Claude Debussy (1862-1918): la musique entière doit être réduite à une combinaison de nombres ; elle est l’arithmétique du son, comme l’optique est la géométrie de la lumière.

(Music is the arithmetic of sounds as optics is the geometry of light.)

Another Google result of the word “sublime” : In aesthetics, the sublime (from the Latin sublīmis) is the quality of greatness, whether physical, moral, intellectual, metaphysical, aesthetic, spiritual, or artistic. The term especially refers to a greatness beyond all possibility of calculation, measurement, or imitation.
