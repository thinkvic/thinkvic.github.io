THE HAPPIEST MAN IN THE HOLIDAY - POEM

Nov 29, 2014

It’s the black Friday.
Everybody is out shopping.
On their face is
unconcealed happiness.
From far away,
“silent night, holy night”
“Christ, the Savior is born.”
Is anybody singing?

Making a turn
from Chestnut to Broad Street.
Oh, an old man was waving his hands.
In front of him is
a red plastic coke cup.
How much money does it hold?
He should care,
but he was absorbed
in his own voice.
What did he see?
I am not quite sure,
but he surely saw something
in the night sky.

I guess, 100 years ago,
at this street corner,
a little match girl was here,
selling her matches.
I guess, this moment,
in this year’s holiday,
you both are
the happiest persons.
“silent night, holy night,”
“sleep in heavenly peace.”

PS: This poem was inspired by this old man’s loud and penetrating song on the street when I passed him by during this year’s Thanksgiving season. It could be heard from far away. I think it conveyed happiness to everybody.

Albert Camus said:

“When you have once seen the glow of happiness on the face of a beloved person, you know that a man can have no vocation but to awaken that light on the faces surrounding him. In the depth of winter, I finally learned that within me there lay an invincible summer.”
