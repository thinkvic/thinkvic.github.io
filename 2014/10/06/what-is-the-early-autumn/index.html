WHAT IS THE EARLY AUTUMN? - POEM

Oct 6, 2014

What’s the early autumn?
it’s in the color of the leaves.
the amount of red and yellow
is perfect,
balancing with the green
to make an interesting image.

What’s the early autumn?
it’s in the mature chestnuts.
the smooth and maroon skin
is perfect,
waiting for lucky people
to come and appreciate.

What’s the early autumn?
it’s in the golden rays of the setting sun,
the golden column’s art deco
is perfect,
dancing with the falling leaves
in the shadow and the wind.

the color, perfect,
the smell, perfect,
yet do you feel depressed?
all these are short,
all these are fading,
and all these will be bald.

What’s the early autumn?
It’s in people’s clothes.
A T-shirt is simply not enough.
The night’s chill is instructing:
everyday is a good day.
perfection is an illusion way!

About this poem:
Philadelphia’s autumn comes earlier than the South. It actually started about 1 months ago.
This past summer was simply too good to be true. Very mild. The unknown yellow flower was short-lived but still in my memory.
However, the recent Autumn weather is objectively the best. I was inspired by the wonderful weather recently and also its ephemeral nature.
