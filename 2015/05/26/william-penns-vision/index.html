William Penn's Vision

2015-05-26 phi-art

William Penn, who was born about 2 years later than Issac Newton, was the founder of Pennsylvania and the early city planner of Philadelphia.

Everybody who visits Philadelphia will see his bronze statue at the top of city hall. City hall is the tallest masonry building in the world (548 feet, 167m). If you stand on the ground, the statue at the top looks so small.

Interestingly, everyone who really stands before the statue will be amazed by the size of it because it’s 38 feet tall, which is as high as a 4 story house. The statue is made by Alexander Milne Calder.

William Penn’s vision is far because he stands on top of a 500-foot-tall structure. His idea of constructing two 100-foot-wide main streets is forward looking.

Our vision is small because we stand on the ground and we are constantly blinded by our vision.
