Maeda's Design Sense

Aug 26, 2015

I came across an article titled “Design Sense” in a FastCompany’s magazine. The topic is about the former president of RISD (Rhode Island School of Design), John Maeda.

Some thoughts are very interesting and I resonated with many of them. I am also constantly inspired by the history of Industrial Revolution, the automobile and computer. For example, from the history of automobile, I can see that without horse carriages, there would not be automobiles!

I also share his idea of “going dark.” We could be deeply trapped!

About that Spring Counter, it’s simple but yet meaningful. I think that’s what most of programs lack these days.

 

Here is what he says:

==Who would be on his list of most creative people?

“Paola Antonelli, senior curator at New York’s Museum of Modern Art. “She predicts the patterns in culture and industry that we need to care about now and in the future.””

==What inspires him?

“History: the history of the Industrial Revolution, the history of the automobile, the history of computer. And it’s excited me to see that these are just patterns.”

==Quirky side project

“I wrote a program called Spring Counter. You type in how old you are and it tells you how many springs you have left to live. It shows you all the flowers that are left in your life and the ones that have already died. I find it hugely motivating.”

==How do you stay productive?

Stow your iPhone. “I try to hide, go dark. It’s a great ritual to unplug for 3 hours and delete 5 apps on my phone–so I can stop. Phones are designed to be addictive.”

Break your habits (even good ones) “I constantly reorganize and redesign the system I’m using. I might use Post-its one week, and I might draw on my hands for a while. I just keep changing my routine so I will be more aware.“
